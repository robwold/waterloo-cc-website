Frequently asked questions

New to the club or thinking of visiting? Here are the things people most often ask us.

Can I bring my child?

Children are welcome to attend, but we do ask that a parent or guardian accompanies them. As the club is based in licensed premises and runs until late in the evening, the children's chess club in Crosby library after school might be a more suitable alternative for many.

An adult's chess club is also in operation on Monday afternoons.

What does it cost?Draft

Coming along on a Monday evening to play is free — we simply ask that you buy a drink at the bar, as the venue kindly hosts us.

Members who play in the leagues pay a small annual subscription, which covers Merseyside Chess Association registration and match costs.

How does league and casual chess work?Draft

There are two sides to a typical evening. Competitive league matches are played at classical time controls in the Merseyside Chess Association — our first team competes in Division 2 and our second team in Division 3.

Alongside the league games, members and visitors can usually find a casual, faster-paced game if they'd rather just play for fun. If you'd like a rated league game, have a word with the team captain and we'll find you a board.
